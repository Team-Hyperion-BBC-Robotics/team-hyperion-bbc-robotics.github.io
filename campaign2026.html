Our Mission

BACK ON THE WORLD STAGE

After an incredible 2025 campaign in Salvador, Brazil — where we placed 14th individually and earned 1st in the Lightweight SuperTeam — Team Hyperion is preparing for an even more ambitious challenge: the 2026 RoboCup Junior World Championship in Incheon, South Korea.

We returned to Australia from Salvador and went undefeated at the 2025 Australian National Championship, reclaiming our country's qualification spot and proving we are still the benchmark for Lightweight Soccer in Australia.

Now the hard work begins again. We are upgrading our hardware, refining our strategy algorithms, and building on everything we learned in Salvador. Incheon is where we take the next step.

Development

WHAT WE'RE IMPROVING

Every World Championship teaches us something new. We are taking every lesson from Salvador and turning it into a concrete upgrade for Incheon.

⚙️
Mechanical
Refined chassis geometry, improved kicker mechanism, and updated dribbling system for tighter ball control under pressure.
⚡
Electrical
Upgraded PCB design with lower noise floor, improved power distribution, and updated motor driver configuration for faster response.
🧠
Software
Enhanced ball-tracking algorithms, improved role-switching logic between attacker and defender, and faster sensor fusion pipeline.
📡
Communication
Improved Bluetooth inter-robot communication for better coordination, with faster handshake times and more reliable packet delivery.
👁️
Vision
Camera system tuning for varied lighting conditions — a key lesson from international competition where court lighting differed from training.
🎯
Strategy
New offensive patterns and set-piece positioning built on analysis of our Salvador matches and footage from top-ranking international teams.

The Destination

INCHEON SOUTH KOREA

Incheon is a city at the frontier of technology and innovation — an ideal backdrop for the world's premier robotics student competition. Located just west of Seoul, Incheon is South Korea's third-largest city and a gateway to one of Asia's most technologically advanced nations.

For Team Hyperion, competing in South Korea represents more than just another international trip. South Korea has a world-renowned robotics and engineering culture, and going back stronger from our 2025 experience means everything to us.

We are grateful to Brisbane Boys' College and our families for making this journey possible, and we are committed to representing them with the same hunger and professionalism we brought to Salvador.
